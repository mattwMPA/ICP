## Summary

This persona centres on **smaller, growth-stage consumer/lifestyle brands** where Amazon is positioned as a primary acquisition and growth channel rather than a channel-conflict problem. Across the three accounts the same archetype emerges: a **founder-led or founder-adjacent commercial owner** (often the founder themselves, an e-com lead reporting directly to founders/VC, or a head of e-commerce inside a VC-rolled-up portfolio) who treats Amazon as a strategic growth bet. The product is genuinely differentiated (artisan coffee, premium hair styling, natural pet treats), the brand has organic momentum (social, retail, footballer endorsements, retail listings), and the team is bootstrapped or capital-disciplined. Compared to the Heritage Manufacturers persona, these prospects worry less about channel conflict and more about **new-to-brand acquisition, ad efficiency, content/digital-shelf execution, and scaling without burning capital**. The pain is universal: a previous agency or in-house attempt has either burnt through ad spend with no NTB to show for it, or has failed to push the right hero SKU. The opportunity for Optimizon is to be the **strategic partner that proves a profitable acquisition motion**, with full-funnel ad activation (DSP where appropriate), a digital-shelf refresh, and reporting that survives both founder scrutiny and (in Assisi's case) VC scrutiny.

**Key profile bullets:**
- UK-based growth-stage consumer/lifestyle brands; either founder-led (92 Degrees, BarberNo1) or VC-backed buy-and-build (Assisi)
- Premium or differentiated product positioning — vegan/sugar-free/kosher (BarberNo1); single-origin artisan coffee with retail roastery (92 Degrees); natural pet treats with strong category share (Assisi)
- Predominantly **Seller (3P) and FBA**, with Assisi running a hybrid Vendor + FBA + 3P mix; Amazon is positioned as a growth and acquisition channel
- Revenue range: small (BarberNo1 ~1k–1.4k units/month before scale; 92 Degrees with no Amazon presence yet) to substantial (Assisi ~£2.1m+ vendor sell-in)
- Decision-maker is **the founder, founder-adjacent, or a head of e-com directly answerable to founders / VC backers** — not buried in a corporate matrix
- Primary pains: previous agency under-delivered on NTB / wasted ad spend; weak digital shelf or under-pushed hero SKU; thin internal team; tight margin discipline; in Assisi's case post-acquisition integration debt and VC pressure
- Decision criteria: agency strategic credibility, full-funnel capability (incl. DSP), tight ad-spend discipline, NTB measurement, transparency, and partnership feel
- Budget profile: **bootstrapped / staged** — willing to scale ad spend if the early KPIs land; not willing to burn capital on vanity metrics
- Aspirations: become category leader; cross-channel orchestration (D2C, retail, Amazon); for VC-backed Assisi, hit return milestones and consolidate brand portfolio

## Recommendations

**1. Lead with NTB measurement, not gross-sales projections.**
Every prospect in this group has either been burnt by, or fears being burnt by, an agency that delivers units without delivering new customers. Optimizon's pitch should start with a clear NTB measurement framework (Nozzle cohort tracking, S&S retention rates, lapsed-cohort reactivation maths) — and the conversation about ROAS/ACOS should be subordinated to that. This addresses BarberNo1's stated trauma directly and equips Trevor at Assisi for VC conversations.

**2. Identify and unlock the under-pushed hero SKU early.**
BarberNo1's Boost Powder is the clearest case — retail bestseller, zero Amazon push. Assisi's Hollings Sausages and Pet Munchies cases are equivalents. 92 Degrees doesn't have a hero SKU on Amazon yet, but the equivalent move is "what's the best starter SKU that converts a sample into a buyer?" In every case, the diagnostic should surface a concrete under-leveraged asset within the first 60 days. This is the single most repeatable proof-point in this archetype.

**3. Bundle the Smolen group capability where it adds genuine fit.**
92 Degrees lit up specifically when Send Me a Sample was introduced; the discovery → sample → Amazon → retargeting motion is a genuine differentiator that Amazon-only agencies can't match. For BarberNo1 (4M social views/month), DSP retargeting via PackView is the natural extension. For Assisi, the breadth around DTC, retail-field, and B2B may be relevant as inhousing accelerates. Use the group story selectively — don't lead with it for prospects where Amazon execution is the primary need.

**4. Use staged commercial models — quarterly review gates work.**
BarberNo1 negotiated quarterly review gates and a 6-month break clause. That is the right shape for bootstrapped or capital-disciplined consumer brands and should be Optimizon's default offer for this archetype, not the exception. It de-risks both sides and signals confidence.

**5. Solve the operational basics before scaling spend.**
92 Degrees needs Square ↔ Amazon connectivity scoped before any meaningful ad campaign. BarberNo1 has product licensing and influencer-asset gaps. Assisi has a multi-thousand-asset creative backlog. Don't push for ad scale until the digital shelf is at a defensible baseline — these prospects will (rightly) judge the agency on whether it forces good operations or just runs ads.

**6. Founder / VC reporting is a deliverable, not an afterthought.**
For founder-led brands (BarberNo1, 92 Degrees), reporting must be readable to a non-marketer who has to defend the spend internally. For Assisi, reporting must satisfy a VC audience focused on NTB, retention, and contribution margin. In both cases this is an explicit deliverable in the engagement design — produce a board-ready scorecard, not just a marketing dashboard.

**7. Re-engagement on dormant prospects: act on signal, not silence.**
BarberNo1 is 167 days silent. Silence in this archetype most likely means heads-down execution, not lost interest — but it's worth a structured re-engagement (data check-in, scorecard, next-quarter reset) rather than waiting for the prospect to come back. Don't infer churn risk from silence alone; verify.

**8. Green flags for this archetype:**
- Founder-led or founder-adjacent decision making (fast cycles)
- Strong off-Amazon brand equity (social, retail, sponsorships) under-leveraged on Amazon
- Premium / differentiated positioning, not commodity
- Clear NTB acquisition framing — they ask about new customers, not just sales
- Existing recurring-revenue motion (Subscribe & Save, app subscriptions, D2C subs) to layer on top
- Capital discipline rather than no-budget; staged commitment willingness

**9. Red flags to watch:**
- No Amazon revenue base (92 Degrees) — long sales cycle on first commercial decision
- Long silence post-onboarding without active reach-out (BarberNo1 — needs re-engagement)
- VC-imposed tools / partners diluting agency scope (Assisi: Profitero, inhousing of Criteo/CitrusAd)
- Multiple operational migrations competing with the Amazon project for bandwidth (92 Degrees: Square + franchising)
- Multi-agency split with consolidation politics (Assisi)
- Inherited brand-relaunch disasters that need ad spend just to repair, not to grow (Assisi High Life)
