## Summary

This persona centres on **growth-stage and growth-phase brands building a multi-channel Amazon presence** — the connective theme is "we have a brand and a product set already working somewhere; now we need to launch or scale on Amazon in the next target market(s) without breaking the rest of the channel mix." Puggle is launching a nursery brand on Amazon UK against an existing D2C/website, with a 100-SKU catalog and an active warehouse-tech integration in flight. SloJo is a South-African designer-drinks B2B leader expanding into US (live, $10k/month early traction) and UK (pre-launch, Costa/Greggs in talks), looking to consolidate a fragmented agency stack into a single sister-company-led partnership. YETI is the most mature: UK Amazon already at £10m+, scaling to €18.9m UK and €5.6m DE in 2026 against a €130m EU 2026 target, with explicit 100% YoY ambition where the *unlock* is cracking Germany. Across all three, the commercial conversation is dominated by **operational lift before scale** (catalog setup, content syndication, brand-tone localisation, fulfilment configuration including SFP/FBA/3PL), an **efficient launch playbook**, and **performance-based or hybrid commercial terms** that align agency incentives with growth.

**Key profile bullets:**
- Brand + product foundation already proven in at least one market (D2C, B2B, or another geography); Amazon is the *next channel* or *next market*, not the foundation
- Active range expansion or new-market entry: 100 SKUs (Puggle UK launch), ~6 SKUs scaling toward broader US range plus UK launch (SloJo), DE/FR/ES/NL/IE expansion + bags/cookware/cargo verticals (YETI)
- D2C + Marketplace mindset: every prospect explicitly framed Amazon as one channel inside a wider mix, not the whole business
- Strong views on **brand integrity** and **price/discount discipline** (especially YETI and SloJo) — Amazon must work without becoming the discount channel
- Onboarding-heavy posture: first 60–90 days dominated by template / catalog / asset / fulfilment integration; scaling spend comes *after* the foundation is in place
- Decision-makers are commercially strong, often founder-adjacent or director-level (Saj Sana = Director with vendor-side history; SloJo's Russ = founder; Puggle's "Eddie/director" through O4b Boardroom mic)
- Commercial preference: **performance-tied retainers or hybrid retainer + commission** (SloJo and YETI both probed this explicitly), not flat-fee project work
- Geographic ambition exceeds current setup capability — needs partner with multi-market footprint and native-language capability (German specifically called out by YETI; UK/EU by SloJo; UK-only for Puggle but Manchester field-presence and SFP-capable warehousing required)

## Recommendations

**1. Lead with the onboarding playbook, not the growth pitch.**
Every prospect in this cohort opened with operational anxiety — the template, the SKU count, the warehouse plumbing, the catalog work. The sales conversation that converts here is "here's exactly what days 1–90 look like, exactly what we need from you, exactly when listings go live, exactly what your team does vs what we do." The growth strategy slide deck is for week 4, not week 1.

**2. Name the team and own continuity from the first call.**
YETI's Bristol induction explicitly walked through every named team member; Puggle wanted Joe (account manager) and the content PM up to Manchester in person; SloJo wanted "single point of contact" repeatedly. These prospects buy people, not capability decks. Bring named senior account manager + content lead + paid media lead to the discovery itself when possible.

**3. Bring native-language capability into the room early — German specifically.**
YETI made a German-native team member a *decision criterion*, not a nice-to-have. Anyone pitching this archetype into Europe needs to lead with the language fluency and local-market evidence. For US-headquartered prospects (SloJo's parent ecosystem; YETI's US gravity) this is the single biggest differentiator over US-based agencies.

**4. Offer hybrid commercials with a baseline carve-out.**
YETI explicitly objected to paying commission on the existing £10m baseline. SloJo wanted retainer-heavy upfront with a switch to commission later. Lead with: low-baseline retainer + commission on incremental revenue above an agreed baseline, scaled by territory. Don't lead with flat retainer or % of total — those will both be objected to by this archetype.

**5. Treat "extension of the team" / "no agency word" language as load-bearing.**
This is a stated value across all three prospects. The pitch should drop the word "agency" wherever possible and lean into "growth partner / extension of your team." YETI's Bristol meeting effectively was an internal team induction — the agency boundary was deliberately erased.

**6. For multi-market plays, sequence the markets and prove the lead market first.**
YETI Germany, SloJo UK, Puggle UK-only — every prospect has a *primary* market where they want clear evidence before a multi-market rollout. The pitch should sequence: month 1–3 lead market foundations; month 3–6 lead market growth; month 6–12 next market activation. Bundling all markets at once will kill the trust-build moment.

**7. Build the brand-tone protection conversation upfront.**
YETI on poetic copy in German; SloJo on B2B-vs-D2C voice; Puggle on brand deck and creative team. None of these prospects will accept generic agency listings. Show wireframe + brand-deck-led examples in the pitch, not generic Amazon-listing thumbnails.

**8. Green flags to look for in this archetype:**
- Product/brand foundation already proven in at least one market (D2C, B2B, or another geography)
- Active range expansion (50+ SKUs to onboard or in-pipeline)
- D2C site running (and ideally outperforming Amazon today, so Amazon = additive, not substitutional)
- Director-level or founder-level decision-maker with agency-side empathy
- Performance-aligned commercial appetite; willingness to discuss retainer + commission hybrids
- Stated geographic ambition that exceeds current capability (multi-market roadmap)
- "No discount" or "premium positioning" stance that protects margin economics

**9. Red flags to watch:**
- Single discovery call only (Puggle, SloJo) — proceed with caution; build a second-call-soon expectation into the close
- Multi-stakeholder approval chain spanning continents (YETI: US VP + procurement + Saj; SloJo: ZA + US partner)
- Existing agency in another geography that *could* take the work (YETI Optimizons US is the canonical case — be ready for the geo-vs-incumbent conversation)
- Resource turnover or strain on the prospect side (Puggle marketing team -2; YETI Amazon team of 4 stretched)
- Brand-asset rights complexity (YETI's licensed video content with expiry dates) — agency must show awareness of asset clearance
- "We'll just build it in-house with offshore back-office" alternative on the table (YETI Pakistan playbook) — pitch must be price-defensible against this baseline

**10. Reporting depth is a buying signal — show it before being asked.**
Saj Sana explicitly said reporting was below his standard at YETI today. The 17-step audit walkthrough James did with Saj on 21/04/2026 visibly resonated. Lead the discovery with the audit deliverable framing — top-line forecasting, ASIN-level profitability, traffic + conversion metrics, ad allocation, market-share analysis, opportunity gaps — not with services brochure pages.
