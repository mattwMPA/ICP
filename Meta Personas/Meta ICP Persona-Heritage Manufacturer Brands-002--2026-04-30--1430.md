## Summary

This persona centres on **established heritage manufacturers** of physical goods — bathroom fixtures, kitchen sinks, hand tools, sports equipment — selling on Amazon as one channel among many. Across all five accounts the same archetype emerges: a **lone commercial or e-commerce leader** (typically managing director, head of online, or e-com manager) inside a complex distribution business, trying to grow Amazon meaningfully despite a vendor relationship that erodes price control, an incumbent agency or internal team that is operational rather than strategic, content cloned or imported from another market, channel-conflict tension with traditional retail/distributor partners, and a budget that is either capped, unsigned, or has to be defended internally. The Amazon revenue picture is almost universally **declining or stagnant** even where overall brand health is fine, which is what triggers the Optimizon conversation. The opportunity is to position Optimizon as the **strategic partner** that reclaims pricing control via a hybrid 1P+3P model, rebuilds content to local-market standard, and reports in a way that satisfies finance and senior stakeholders.

**Key profile bullets:**
- UK or Europe-based heritage manufacturers; long-established brands with strong recognition in their category
- Predominantly **vendor (1P)** today — most are exploring or partway through a transition to a hybrid 1P+3P model
- Amazon revenue typically £400k–£10m and **flat or declining year-on-year** (Aqualisa down ~30% YoY; Draper UK down from £6.5m to £4.4m on vendor)
- Premium or specialist positioning — these brands are rarely the cheapest in their category and feel particularly exposed to Amazon's price-pressure dynamics
- Decision-maker is a **single senior commercial owner** (MD, Head of Online, E-com Manager) often working with a thin team and reporting upward to finance, parent group, or in some cases an overseas HQ
- Primary pains: pricing erosion, channel conflict with own distributors/retailers, weak/unlocalised content, lack of strategic agency input, inventory/forecasting gaps, internal politics around treating Amazon as a real channel
- Budget profile: **cautious and defensible** — £4.5–10k/month retainer territory, often with finance gatekeeping; ad spend has to be justified against ROAS not just gross sales
- Decision criteria: strategic depth, proactivity, hybrid model expertise, ability to operate across multiple EU markets, and ability to report in a way that satisfies finance/parent group

## Recommendations

**1. Lead with the hybrid 1P+3P thesis.**
Every prospect in this group has the same fundamental pain: vendor erodes pricing control, and they've either tried and failed to set up Seller Central or haven't started. Optimizon's hybrid model — vendor-as-volume + seller/FBA-as-control + bundles-as-cannibalisation-safe-growth — is the single strongest pitch hook. Use the **Everlast bundle strategy** as a real, working case study in the cannibalisation conversation.

**2. Expect channel-conflict objections — bring the answer pre-loaded.**
Trade distributors and retail partners actively resist Amazon for these brands. Draper's Eurocar Parts story and Everlast's Decathlon caution are typical, not edge cases. Optimizon should walk into discovery with the channel-management framing: pricing discipline, exclusive-on-Amazon variants, bundles, MAP-equivalent strategies, and "Amazon as the price anchor" narrative.

**3. Build the finance / parent-group story early.**
Budgets are scrutinised, sign-offs are upstream, and Aqualisa, Draper, Blanco and Harrow all have a finance- or HQ-level approval chain. Optimizon should propose a scorecard-first reporting cadence designed for a CFO/finance audience — not just a marketing dashboard. Tier commercial models (e.g. lower retainer + commission on incremental revenue) so the champion has something defensible to present.

**4. Watch for the "stalled previous agency / incumbent failure" pattern.**
Aqualisa lost a year on Seller Central setup; Draper is questioning Rhythm's strategic input; Blanco has no agency input at all in the UK. The pitch needs to **prove velocity in the first 60–90 days** — set up Seller Central, fix top-10-by-revenue listings, run a price-control diagnostic, deliver first scorecard. Quick visible wins kill the "it'll be the same as last time" objection.

**5. Range and content investment — sequence carefully.**
Aqualisa is mid-range-rationalisation (EU Drinking Water Directive); Blanco needs 850 SKUs localised; Harrow wants 10–20 SKUs ported from US. Don't sell big content-rebuild engagements upfront — sell **diagnostic + minimum viable optimisation** on existing content, then time the heavy investment to coincide with the brand's own launch window.

**6. Use the Smolen group story for cross-channel prospects.**
Harrow Sports lit up specifically when Juan introduced the wider Smolen group capability (IMA, B2B, D2C). For prospects with a clear "Amazon is one of several growth channels" mindset (Harrow, Draper, Aqualisa multi-brand), pulling in the wider group capability is a meaningful differentiator over Amazon-only agencies.

**7. Green flags to look for in this archetype:**
- Heritage manufacturer with strong brand recognition in category
- Vendor revenue **declining**, not flat-and-comfortable (declining = budget-justifiable urgency)
- Single senior commercial owner with autonomy on Amazon (MD, Head of Online, e-com Manager)
- Existing distributor/retail tension that motivates Amazon as a control lever
- Mid-to-premium price points (£20+ retail) — sub-£20 economics rarely work
- Already attempting (or failing at) Seller Central — proves they understand the problem

**8. Red flags to watch:**
- No hard revenue targets (Blanco) — long sales cycle, low urgency
- Single discovery call only (Blanco, Harrow) — thin signal; treat synthesis cautiously
- Parent-company HQ controls advertising (Blanco) or vendor terms (Aqualisa) — limits agency lever
- Account team transition mid-engagement (Everlast) — relationship risk
- Range mid-replacement (Aqualisa) — content investment ROI is constrained
- Internal political resistance to Amazon as a "real" channel (Draper UK team) — long internal change curve

**9. Team continuity is a stated and recurring concern.**
Everlast just lost Dan Walsh; the conversation explicitly addressed transition. Aqualisa's incumbent failure was about turnover and disengagement. Make team continuity visible in pitches — name the day-to-day account lead, name the strategic counterpart, and own the transition narrative if (when) it changes.
