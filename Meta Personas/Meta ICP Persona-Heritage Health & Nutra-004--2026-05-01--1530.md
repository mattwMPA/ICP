## Summary

This persona centres on **heritage health and nutraceutical brands** — supplements, sports nutrition, sleep & breathing aids, omega-3 — selling on Amazon in vendor or hybrid mode, often as one channel inside a wider professional/retail/distributor mix. Across all four prospects the same archetype recurs: a **single commercial owner** (national account manager, head of e-com, senior brand manager, or founding partner) trying to grow Amazon despite an incumbent agency that is **over-indexed on one lever** (Brain Labs is "ads-only" since the Molzi acquisition; UGA's agency is over-spending DSP and under-spending search; Health Medica's Amazon Strategic Services has been a pain on advertising for years; Longevity tested an external one-man-show on a single SKU). Catalogue is messy (multi-brand under one parent ASIN, content not localised, variations split when they should be grouped), the funnel-mix is wrong (DSP without search, or search without DSP/AMC), and the strategic conversation has been replaced by an operational one. Vendor pressure from Amazon is forcing every prospect to evaluate hybrid or pure seller. They want **a real strategic partner** with end-to-end capability (catalog + content + ads + DSP + AMC + ops + multi-market expansion), measurable margin/EBITDA discipline, and credibility in the regulated supplement/health space.

**Key profile bullets:**
- UK or Europe-based heritage supplement / nutra / sports-nutrition brands; long brand histories (USN 25+ years, Health Medica with strong HCP recommendation flywheel, Longevity built during COVID, UGA omega-3 specialist with Italian pharmaceutical heritage)
- Predominantly **vendor (1P)** today (USN, Health Medica, Longevity); UGA on seller. All actively evaluating hybrid 1P+3P
- Amazon UK revenue ranges: ~£1.5m (Longevity, Health Medica) up to ~£6m ship-cogs (USN); Italy ~€1m+ ad spend (UGA, indicates much larger turnover)
- Multi-market ambition: UK + EU expansion (Health Medica EU plans implicit; Longevity launching US + Australia, EU red-tape blocked; UGA expanding from Italy/Spain into DE/UK/US; USN scaling from UK into EU5)
- Decision-maker is a **single senior commercial owner** managing Amazon either alone or with a thin team; reports upward to founder, MD, or partner group
- Primary pains: incumbent agency narrowed in scope (ads-only or DSP-only), category compliance restrictions, catalog/variation issues, healthcare/professional-channel conflict and cannibalisation worry, funnel-mix imbalance, lack of LTV/CAC visibility on Amazon, expansion friction (EU regulation, Trump tariffs, distributor red tape)
- Budget profile: **mid-tier and growth-justified** — ad spend £20k/mo (USN, ~31x ROAS) up to £25k/mo (Longevity); UGA's annual Italian ad spend ~€1m; willing to scale spend if growth plan is credible
- Decision criteria: hybrid 1P+3P expertise, DSP + AMC + Nozzle (LTV/CAC) capability, full-funnel ads management with strategic depth, multi-market and language localisation, in-house creative + catalog + ops, SmolEn-group cross-channel (Shopify, TikTok Shop, IMA creative)

## Recommendations

**1. Lead with "the strategic-partner-not-just-ad-agency" thesis.**
Every prospect in this group is leaving (or has just left) an agency because the conversation became ads-only. Use Brain Labs (USN), Amazon Strategic Services (Health Medica), and the third-party single-SKU operator (Longevity) as concrete pattern matches. The opening hook is: "Your incumbent has narrowed to one lever — we're a full-stack growth partner across catalog, content, search, DSP, AMC, ops, and cross-channel." Bring the audit-first close (Optimizon offered audits on every call in this cohort): NDA → 2-week audit → present findings → close.

**2. Lead the funnel-mix conversation with data.**
UGA articulated it most sharply: _"We have underspent in sponsored ads and we overspent in DSP."_ This is a pattern, not a UGA-specific problem. Walk into discovery with a diagnostic framework: search-share-of-voice + DSP impact + AMC multi-touch + Nozzle LTV/CAC. Show that you'll re-balance the funnel against the brand's stated goal (revenue vs ROAS vs growth) — and that you'll align stakeholders to a single goal first (Longevity's three-partner mis-alignment is the cautionary tale).

**3. Make the hybrid-1P+3P recommendation the headline of any first deck.**
USN explicitly named this; Health Medica is wrestling with vendor-only AVS; Longevity has a working hybrid template (Summer Escapes flip — internal Optimizon insight). Pre-load the item-level economics framework: "we'll tell you per SKU which platform makes more money for you" — this is the language Joe Smith used.

**4. Use Nozzle / AMC / Pacvue as proof points, not features.**
UGA was an existing Nozzle user — that's the warm path. USN flagged AMC explicitly; PJ's medical-tech background means he'll evaluate it seriously. Longevity already had Nozzle and lost continuity; re-introducing it landed positively. Health Medica is unfamiliar but receptive. Lead with the LTV/CAC + cohort + customer-journey story, then back into the technology.

**5. Bring SmolEn group cross-channel proactively.**
Health Medica lit up on Shopify and TikTok Shop. USN's PJ flagged TikTok unprompted. Longevity's Chris asked "how does it work with these other channels?" The cross-channel story is a meaningful differentiator over Amazon-only competitors (Brain Labs, niche health agencies). Explicitly name IMA, eComplete, and B2B/VTEX capability — it answers the "we don't want to manage four agencies" tension.

**6. Address the agency-acquisition-drift objection head-on.**
Joe Smith (USN) named Brain Labs / Molzi explicitly as a concern. Optimizon's response — "Smolen is fresh, growing, the digital commerce arm is 2% of the parent and *we* are driving it" — landed cleanly on that call and should be standard for any RFP that includes Brain Labs, Tinuiti, Acadia, Stella Rising, or any other recently-acquired agency.

**7. Compliance fluency must be visible.**
Health Medica explicitly named compliance as a deal-breaker for past agencies. Longevity has an active lawsuit (internal Optimizon insight, not a prospect quote). USN is FMCG with claims compliance. Walk into discovery with named case studies in supplements / fertility / sports nutrition / regulated categories — Optimizon mentioned working with one supplement brand on the Health Medica call but didn't name it. Build a 3–5 brand reference deck specifically for this cohort.

**8. Commercial flexibility — pre-load the playbook.**
Every prospect in this group asked about commercials in detail. Optimizon's standard response (retainer + commission, flexible framing — total sales for small businesses, incremental sales for larger ones; market-tiered commission for new vs mature markets) landed well. Add: 12-month minimum with a 6-month review break (used on USN call), low incremental fee for adding new markets if same team/products (used on USN call).

**9. Green flags to look for in this archetype:**
- Heritage health / supplement / nutra / sports-nutrition brand with 10+ year history
- Vendor-pressured or seller-stuck with catalog hygiene issues
- Single senior commercial owner (Joe / PJ / Chris / Gentian / Stephanie) with autonomy
- Funnel mix already broken (DSP-heavy or ads-only) — easy diagnostic win
- Multi-market ambition (UK + EU + US / Australia)
- Cross-channel curiosity (Shopify, TikTok Shop, DTC) — SmolEn cross-sell territory
- Existing Nozzle / Pacvue exposure — warm-tech-stack alignment

**10. Red flags to watch:**
- Multi-stakeholder mis-alignment (Longevity 3-partner cautionary tale) — qualify the decision-maker chain early
- Side-gig founders with full-time jobs (Longevity) — bandwidth ceiling on execution
- Single short discovery call (UGA, Health Medica) — thin signal; treat synthesis cautiously and accelerate the audit
- Active RFP with multiple agencies (USN) — competitive deal, in-person pitch is the deciding moment
- Notice period / incumbent transition (Health Medica AVS 30-day notice) — plan a clean Day-0 to Day-90 plan
- Regulatory blockers (Longevity EU medical devices; Trump tariffs on US launches)
- In-house creative or ops teams (USN) — narrower scope, lower retainer ceiling on home market

**11. The Longevity conversion is the playbook.**
Re-read the 06/02/2026 call. Optimizon converted Longevity by reframing the relationship as "full-service strategic partner OR back-end operator — pick one" and tying advertising to the wider growth story (margin, premium re-brand, US/AU expansion, IMA rebrand creative). Use this exact reframe on any prospect where an external advertising operator has been introduced — it cuts cleanly through the "let us keep the other agency for ads" stall.
