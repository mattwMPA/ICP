## Summary

This persona centres on **heritage / premium brands with strong off-Amazon distribution** — fragrance, salon hair-care, sports protection, watches, fine wine, and food-service syrups — where Amazon is one channel among many and **frequently the channel that creates the most pain**. The unifying tension is **channel conflict**: the brand sits inside a long-established trade, retail, salon, B2B, restaurant, or distributor structure whose pricing, margins and exclusivity Amazon disrupts. Resellers undercut RRP, distributors bypass head-office controls, third parties register the brand on Amazon ahead of the brand itself, and the brand's own COGS / margin floor structurally prevents them from competing at Amazon's ASP. Decision-makers are typically owners, founders or senior commercial leaders (or in some cases a small marketing team in a global group) whose Amazon strategy is explicitly to **reclaim control as a brand-equity exercise**, not just to chase volume. They are open to hybrid 1P+3P, the Optimizon retail / seller-of-record model, distributor displacement, and "Amazon as price-anchor" framings — but they are conservative on cost, careful around their wider trade ecosystem, and wary of any move that makes Amazon a price-leader against their own retail/distribution partners.

**Key profile bullets:**
- Heritage / premium positioning across diverse categories (fragrance, salon hair-care, sports protection, watches, premium wine, premium syrups)
- Amazon revenue mostly £400k–£5m (Time Products UK ~£3–4m; IBC Simply UK strong; Opro UK ~£3.16m + Maru ~£500k vendor; Milton Lloyd UK Amazon ~£3m COGS while D2C is only £100k; MUK UK c.£67k/month via resellers; ZONIN1821 small)
- Distribution backbone is **off-Amazon** — distributors, salons/barbershops, traditional jewellers, restaurants/on-trade, B2B food-service, supermarkets
- Predominantly **vendor (1P) or vendor+seller hybrid**, with several actively transitioning to seller and/or distributor displacement (Opro→Maru playbook is the template)
- Pain stack: unauthorised resellers / "everyone and his dog selling on Amazon", buy-box loss when external retail partners run promos, RRP enforcement breakdown, distributor-to-Amazon bleed, COGS vs RRP margin compression on EU/US expansion, fragmented multi-brand/multi-SKU portfolios, finance scrutiny on margin

## Recommendations

**1. Lead with the channel-conflict thesis, not the Amazon-growth thesis.**
This cohort's pain is structural, not tactical. The brand is fighting distributors, salons, restaurants, retail partners, and unauthorised resellers simultaneously. Optimizon's pitch must open with: "Your Amazon problem is a channel-control problem. Here's the playbook for taking it back." Use **Opro/Maru as the working case study** — Ts & Cs letter, distributor displacement, vendor-to-seller transition, ~3× revenue uplift on Maru projection. Resellers folding when challenged is the moment that lands.

**2. Bring the retail-model / seller-of-record offer to the front of the pitch.**
For MUK, ZONIN1821, IBC Simply EU, and exploratory Milton Lloyd D2C territories, the **retail model with Optimizon as seller-of-record + consignment stock at 3PL** is the differentiated commercial mechanism. It dissolves the alcohol-licence blocker (ZONIN1821), bypasses the manufacturing-cost-stack on EU (IBC Simply), and lets the brand stay margin-disciplined while Optimizon owns execution risk. Quote target: ~20% net margin to Optimizon (MUK accepted this benchmark).

**3. Build the finance / margin-floor story upfront — every time.**
This cohort has FDs, owners, or board-level scrutiny. **Lead financial conversations with the margin floor**, not the revenue ceiling. IBC Simply Michael's "minimum 10% net retail" is the template.
